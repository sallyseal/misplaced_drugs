About PIRATE

PIRATE is a comprehensive Drug-Target resource containing structural, clinical and pharmacological information on promiscuous drugs and their targets. All drugs listed in this database bind at least two targets and only targets with representitive structures in the Protein Data Bank are included. Our database contains 311 unique drug entires and 227 non-redundant targets. For more information on how the database was compiled see the pipeline below. Our database is available for download in the navigation bar.

PIRATE was created in affiliation with Imperial College London

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my drug or target not listed?

The presence of a drug in this database requires that it is promiscuous and that structural information is available for its targets. Or in practical terms, at least two of its targets must be structurally represented in the PDB. Structures are considered to be representitive of a protein if they have at least 60% sequence identity with the protein's sequence on UniProt. Similarly, targets are only present if they bind one of these drugs and have a representitive structure in the PDB.

Why is there no binding site comparison data for my drug?

Due to a variety of factors, binding site comparison data was only successfully generated for a subset of the drugs in this database. This is primarily due to the comparison algorithms' requirements for a certain level of binding site similarity between two proteins.

Why are these two comparisons using the same structures?

The structures used in this database are only representitive of their associated targets. In cases where a drug binds multiple very similar proteins, it is possible for the same structure to be used to represent more than one of these proteins.

Our Pipeline

This figure shows how the database was compiled. Drugs were identified on DrugBank V5.0. Only drugs that had a representitive sturucture of their clinical targets available in the Protein Data Bank were kept. Only drugs that bound to more than 1 of these targets was included in the final database.
